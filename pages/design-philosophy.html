Version 2 of 3

Foundation Edge

Mphasis F1 Foundation as primary identity — technology-forward, confident, bold. The gradient arrow from the F1 logo becomes the design motif. The anti-slop weapon: geometric sans-serif at scale, zero-radius hero elements, and a dark-first layout that means business.

01 — Brand Inspiration

The F1 Gradient as Design System

The Mphasis F1 Foundation logo contains a gradient arrow that transitions from magenta (#D94F8A) through teal (#2DB5A3) to green (#5AC16B). Most implementors treat this as a logo element. Version 2 treats it as a design system.

The gradient appears as a 4px rule below the hero, as the active state underline in the navigation, as section dividers, and as card accent borders. It never appears as a fill — only as a line or border. This restraint prevents it from becoming garish while making it unmistakably present. A visitor who has seen the F1 logo will recognise the connection immediately.

The primary colour is Mphasis sky blue (#009BDE) — the exact colour of "FOUNDATION" in the logo. The background shifts to a very light blue-white (#F0F7FC) in light sections and a deep near-black (#060D17) in the hero and dark sections. This colour palette is consistent with technology companies that use blue as their primary identity colour — but the gradient motif gives it unique fingerprinting.

02 — Typography

Space Grotesk — Geometric, Tech-Forward

Space Grotesk (Florian Karsten, 2018) is a proportional sans-serif designed for technical and digital contexts. It has strong geometric structure, slightly quirky details in letterforms like 'a', 'g', and 't', and performs exceptionally well at large display sizes — particularly for headlines that need to project confidence.

Unlike Inter (the body font, which is retained for its excellent screen legibility), Space Grotesk has a distinct personality. The combination of Space Grotesk headlines over Inter body text creates hierarchy through contrast of personality, not just weight. Both fonts are geometric sans-serifs, which creates visual cohesion while maintaining the distinction between display and text roles.

The typographic scale is enlarged: --text-6xl is 4.5rem instead of the original 3.75rem. On a dark hero, large type looks authoritative rather than oversized. This is an intentional departure from the cautious sizing of the original.

03 — Layout & Space

Dark-First. Sharp. Wider.

The hero section is dark by default (#060D17) — a deliberate inversion of the light-first original. Dark-first layouts communicate confidence. They are also significantly harder to execute well than light-first designs, which is why they signal design investment. Getting dark mode right requires calibrating text contrast ratios, shadow visibility, and component depth in a way that light mode forgives.

Zero border radius on hero elements is the other signature decision. Cards, buttons, and hero containers have 0–2px radius throughout. This is not minimalism for its own sake — it is a typographic decision. Sharp corners reinforce the geometric character of Space Grotesk and create visual alignment between the typography and the container shapes.

The container is widened to 1480px (--container-2xl) to match the confident scale of the typography. On large screens, the site fills the frame rather than floating in the centre with excessive negative space.

05 — Design Craft

What Makes This Not AI Slop

  • 01
    The F1 gradient is a design system, not a decoration The gradient appears in exactly four places: hero rule, nav active state, section dividers, card borders. Every instance is a 2–4px line, never a fill. This is a controlled usage system. AI-generated sites use gradients as fills and backgrounds indiscriminately. The constraint is what makes it work.
  • 02
    Zero border radius on hero elements is a typographic decision Space Grotesk has angular geometric letterforms. The zero-radius containers echo this geometry — they are not minimalist, they are consistent. When the containers are rounded, there is a visual contradiction between the sharp type and the soft container. Removing radius resolves the contradiction.
  • 03
    Dark hero requires calibrated component depth On a dark background (#060D17), shadows are invisible. The version removes hero card shadows and instead uses subtle border highlights. This is not laziness — it is the correct technical decision. Shadows only work when they cast against lighter backgrounds. The adaptation demonstrates understanding of the medium.
  • 04
    Makerspace pillar colour changed to Mphasis blue In the original, Makerspace is red — a holdover from the original brand without reasoning. In Version 2, it becomes Mphasis sky blue (#009BDE). This is a deliberate editorial choice: the Makerspace is the most tangible, hands-on expression of the F1 Foundation's mission. It gets the primary brand colour.
  • 05
    Wider container (1480px) matched to the type scale Space Grotesk at 4.5rem display size needs horizontal room. A 1200px container constrains headlines and creates awkward line breaks. The 1480px container lets headlines breathe without forcing them into smaller sizes. Container width and type scale must be designed together — AI generators choose both independently.
